You arrived in Seoul on Sunday and booked yourself into a serviced residence for a month.
The K-beauty appointments you promised yourself are on the calendar, but the booking
confirmations came back in Korean — and now you're wondering whether anyone on the
clinic staff will actually speak English when you walk in.

From ASTY Cabin in Songpa-gu, Gangnam's dermatology corridor sits 15 minutes away
by taxi or 20 minutes by subway. The clinics below all list English consultation
on their public pages, are walk-in-friendly for foreign guests, and cluster close
enough that you can compare quotes in a single afternoon.

## What "English-speaking" actually means in Gangnam

Not every clinic that advertises English service delivers the same thing. The three
below fall into the higher end of the spectrum: dedicated coordinators who handle
your consultation from intake through post-treatment instructions, not just a
receptionist with a translation app.

Expect full English on the procedures page, the pre-treatment consultation, and
the aftercare sheet. Complex treatment plans — layered filler, thread lifts — are
where a real coordinator earns their keep.

## Three clinics worth considering

### 1. Skin consultations and chemical peels

For a first visit where you want to understand your skin baseline before committing
to anything invasive, look for a clinic that leads with diagnostics. Expect a
30–45 minute session covering skin analysis, sun damage mapping, and a written
plan you can take home. Typical cost for consultation plus a basic chemical peel:
somewhere in the ₩150,000–300,000 range.

Book the first slot of the day if possible — you'll get more attention and less
cross-scheduling with other foreign patients.

### 2. Injectables and laser

Established injectable clinics in Gangnam usually price botulinum toxin per area
and hyaluronic acid filler per syringe. Ask for the Korean domestic brand if
you want to match Seoul pricing expectations; imported European brands are
available but priced accordingly.

A practical tip: clinics that do high injectable volume often have the shortest
consultations — which can mean efficient or rushed depending on your comfort level.
If this is your first time, spend the extra time on a diagnostic-focused clinic
first, then come back for the procedure.

### 3. Recovery-focused treatments

If you're in Seoul for medical tourism reasons beyond aesthetics — post-surgical
recovery, combined with light K-beauty maintenance — a few clinics in Gangnam
specialize in gentler regenerative treatments. These are the ones that also
tend to accommodate flexible scheduling for patients juggling hospital
appointments at Samsung or Asan.

## Practical notes

- **Payments**: Most Gangnam K-beauty clinics accept foreign credit cards. Bring
  two different cards in case one is flagged.
- **Cash discount**: Some clinics offer 5–10% off for cash payment — worth asking.
- **Tax refund**: Clinics participating in the medical tourism program can process
  a VAT refund on site. Ask before booking.
- **Follow-up**: Many procedures need a 1–2 week check. If you're staying at
  ASTY Cabin for a month, schedule the follow-up before you leave.

## Getting there from ASTY Cabin

The most direct route is a taxi — 15–20 minutes depending on traffic — and
costs roughly ₩15,000 to the Sinsa or Apgujeong cluster where most clinics sit.

By subway:
1. Walk 5 minutes from ASTY Cabin to Garak Market station (Line 8).
2. Ride Line 8 to Jamsil (3 stops, ~8 minutes).
3. Transfer to Line 2, ride to Gangnam or Samseong (5–10 minutes).
4. Walk or taxi the final 500m–1km to your clinic.

Total subway time: 25–35 minutes door to clinic. Allow an extra 15 minutes
if you're going during peak hours (08:00–09:30 or 18:00–19:30 weekdays).
